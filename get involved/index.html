Get Involved with City limits

You are an essential part of our effort to shed light on under-reported stories and inform the people who shape the future of New York. Here are ways to be more deeply involved:

Send Us a News Tip

Our readers are our eyes and ears. We always protect your privacy. You can use this form, email us, call 646-693-4459 ext. 1 or mail us at 8 West 126th Street, New York NY 10027.

Ask a Question About New York City

How do you get a problem fixed?Who do you ask about a particular issue? City Limits certainly doesn’t have all the answers. But we have the time and duty to try to track them down. If there’s something you want to know about, fill out the simple form, and we’ll be on it.

Follow us on Social Media

We’re on Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter and YouTube.

Subscribe to Our Newsletters

Our readers are our eyes and ears. We always protect your privacy. You can use this form, email us, call 646-693-4459 ext. 1 or mail us at 8 West 126th Street, New York NY 10027.

Become a Member

Journalism takes work, and work takes money. If you think what we do is important—if it’s had value for you—support us with a donation.

Submit an Op-Ed

We publish CityViews topics on every topic under the New York City sun. And moon. Let us know what’s on your mind.

Apply to be an Intern

City Limits offers paid internships to high-school students through our CLARIFY program; details are here. Occasional one-on-one internships for course credit are arranged on a rolling basis; email us for more information.

City Limits uses investigative journalism through the prism of New York City to identify urban problems, examine their causes, explore solutions, and equip communities to take action.

Founded in 1976 in the midst of New York’s fiscal crisis, City Limits exists to inform democracy and equip citizens to create a more just city. The organization is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit funded by foundation support, ad sponsorship and donations from readers.
